Benchtop Spectrophotometers

Directional (45°/0° and 0°/45°) geometry benchtop spectrophotometers replicate the human visual response to color and include applications where gloss and texture can affect the color measurement.

Portable Spectrophotometer

From shipping departments to storage areas to plant production floors, our portable spectrophotometers allow you to measure on the go for the ultimate testing convenience. Choose from Directional 45°/0° and Diffuse d/8° geometries.

In-Process / In-Line

When installed in a process stream, in-process (in-line) spectrophotometers provide continuous color measurement data throughout a production run, enabling quick responses to color variations.

Quality Control (QC) Software

Color matching software that reports absolute and color-difference data in all widely used color scales under various lighting and observing conditions.

Color & Appearance Measurement Solutions

We usually think of color as a qualitative characteristic — something we determine based on sensory observations. Using spectrophotometer data. This information is useful for precise applications like quality control, biochemical research, and product design.

A spectrophotometer identifies a color's numerical values, giving industries and regulators the necessary tools for:

Color Standards

Using a spectrophotometer, you can establish company benchmarks or meet industry standards and achieve uniform color repeatedly.

Brand Consistency

Your brand colors make you instantly recognizable and help attract and retain loyal customers. The visual appearance of your product is a reflection on your brand.

Quality

Changes in color can reveal when products have gone bad, which is critical for detecting quality, and safety issues in industries such as food and beverage and pharmaceuticals.

Safety

Color measurement helps public safety agencies in various ways, such as developing noticeable traffic signs and identifying key forensic evidence.

Meet Quality Standards

Equip your facility with the best instruments to ensure adherence to critical product quality standards such as:

The American Paper and Pulp Association (TAPPI) sets standards for paper quality, which you can easily verify using a spectrophotometer.

The ASTM publishes standards for many different industries, including textiles, plastics, and paints. By confirming quality through a spectrophotometer, you can ensure your product is up to code.

From testing food safety to testing and calibration, spectrophotometry can help your organization meet many of the International Organization for Standardization's (ISO) quality control standards.
